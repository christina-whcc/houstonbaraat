The Groom's Grand Entrance

The Baraat

The baraat is the groom's moment — the procession that announces his arrival with joy, music, color, and celebration. Traditionally, the groom rides atop the horse himself, elevated above the crowd as family and friends dance around him to the beat of the dhol. It is an entrance that commands the street, moves the heart, and sets the tone for the entire wedding. For families who prefer it, a horse-drawn carriage is also available as an alternative.

The Bride's Farewell

The Vidaai

The vidaai is one of the most tender and meaningful moments in a South Asian wedding — the bride's farewell from her family as she begins her new life. While the baraat is our specialty, we are honored to offer the vidaai as well. A horse-drawn carriage gives this sacred farewell the grace, beauty, and weight it deserves — slowing the moment down and creating space for everything the family is feeling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Houston Families Ask Us

What areas do you serve?

+
We serve all of Greater Houston — including Sugar Land, Cypress, Katy, Pearland, Missouri City, The Woodlands, Stafford, Richmond, Friendswood, and League City. We also serve Austin, San Antonio, and communities across Texas. If you don't see your city listed, contact us — we travel statewide and will do our best to accommodate your date and location.

How far in advance should we book?

+
As early as possible — ideally 3 to 6 months before your wedding date. Houston wedding season fills up quickly, especially spring and fall weekends. Contact us as soon as your date is confirmed.

Does the horse wear a costume?

+
We have a beautiful ceremonial costume imported directly from India — the full traditional look that many families love. However, if the family prefers a horse without a costume, that is absolutely fine too. Just let us know in advance so we can prepare accordingly.

Can we feed the horse during the baraat?

+
Yes — many families love to offer the horse something special, and we welcome it. We just ask that you let us know in advance and discuss what you plan to bring. Some foods are not safe for horses, so a quick conversation ahead of time makes sure everyone — especially the horse — has a great day.

Are your horses calm with dhol and dancing?

+
Yes. Our horse is calm and experienced in the high-energy environment of a baraat — dhol drums, DJ trucks, music, and large crowds. Calm and completely at ease, no matter what the celebration brings.

Can we decorate the horse and carriage?

+
Absolutely. We welcome marigold garlands, floral arrangements, fabric draping, and any decorations the family wants to add. Families often tie their own decorations onto the horse — this is welcome and encouraged. We ask that decorations be passed to us at setup so we can make sure everything is secured safely and looks its best.

Do you also provide carriages for the vidaai?

+
Yes. While the baraat is our specialty and primary service, we do offer horse-drawn carriages for the vidaai — the bride's send-off as she begins her new life. We can coordinate both the baraat and vidaai as a full-day package.

Do you offer other services beyond baraats?

+
Yes — White Horse and Carriage Company offers a full range of horse-drawn carriage services across Texas, including wedding carriages, funeral processions, quinceañeras, parades, and corporate events. Visit whitehorseandcarriage.com to learn more.
